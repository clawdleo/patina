I Don't Flip Bikes.
I Rescue Them.

It started with a rusty Zaskar I found in a barn in Austria. Everyone said strip it, repaint it, make it look new. But something felt wrong about erasing 30 years of history.

That bike had stories in its scratches. Trail dust embedded in places no brush could reach. A dent on the top tube from some forgotten crash in the 90s.

So I cleaned it. Serviced it. Protected the patina. And when I posted it online, something clicked. People didn't want showroom-perfect restorations. They wanted authenticity.
